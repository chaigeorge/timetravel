Ancient
1.8M BCE
Olduvai Gorge
Homo habilis fossils found here — among the oldest evidence of human ancestors on Earth.
Ancient
3.6M BCE
Laetoli Footprints
Australopithecus afarensis left footprints in volcanic ash — the oldest evidence of upright walking.
Ancient
500 BCE
Bantu Migration
Bantu-speaking peoples begin settling the region, bringing iron tools and agriculture.
Trade
100 CE
Periplus Mention
Greek text describes trading ports on the East African coast — Tanzania's coast enters written history.
Trade
800s
Swahili Civilisation
Coastal city-states flourish. Arab, Persian and Indian merchants trade gold, ivory and enslaved people through Zanzibar.
Trade
1498
Vasco da Gama
Portuguese explorer arrives on the coast, disrupting Indian Ocean trade routes for a century.
Trade
1698
Omani Rule Begins
The Sultanate of Oman expels the Portuguese and takes control of Zanzibar, transforming it into a clove empire.
Trade
1840
Sultan Moves to Zanzibar
Sultan Seyyid Said relocates his capital from Muscat to Zanzibar, making it the most powerful city on the East African coast.
Colonial
1858
Burton & Speke Arrive
British explorers reach Lake Tanganyika searching for the source of the Nile, opening the interior to colonial attention.
Colonial
1886
Anglo-German Treaty
Britain and Germany divide East Africa between them. Germany takes mainland Tanganyika; Britain takes Kenya and Zanzibar.
Colonial
1905–07
Maji Maji Rebellion
Tanzanians revolt against German colonial rule. Over 300,000 die, mostly from famine caused by German scorched-earth tactics.
Colonial
1919
British Mandate
After WWI, Germany loses Tanganyika. Britain takes control under a League of Nations mandate.
Colonial
1954
TANU Founded
Julius Nyerere founds the Tanganyika African National Union, the political engine of independence.
Independence
1961
Independence Day
December 9 — Tanganyika becomes independent. Julius Nyerere becomes Prime Minister. The flag is raised for the first time.
Independence
1962
Republic Declared
Tanganyika becomes a republic. Nyerere is elected as the first President, stepping down briefly to focus on building TANU.
Independence
1963
Zanzibar Independence
The Sultanate of Zanzibar gains independence from Britain — but not for long.
Independence
1964
Zanzibar Revolution
January — The Arab Sultan is overthrown in a violent revolution. Abeid Karume leads the new People's Republic of Zanzibar.
Independence
1964
🇹🇿 Tanzania Born
April 26 — Tanganyika and Zanzibar unite to form the United Republic of Tanzania. Nyerere becomes first President.
Modern
1967
Arusha Declaration
Nyerere launches Ujamaa — African socialism. Banks and industries nationalised. Villages collectivised.
Modern
1973
Capital Moved
Nyerere moves the capital from Dar es Salaam to Dodoma — a symbolic break from the colonial coastal capital.
Modern
1979
Uganda War
Tanzania invades Uganda and ousts dictator Idi Amin, liberating Kampala. A rare African military success story.
Modern
1985
Nyerere Steps Down
After 21 years, Nyerere voluntarily retires — one of the few African leaders to peacefully transfer power. Ali Hassan Mwinyi succeeds him.
Modern
1992
Multi-Party Democracy
Tanzania legalises opposition parties after decades of single-party rule, beginning its democratic transition.
Modern
1999
Nyerere Dies
Julius Nyerere, "Mwalimu" (The Teacher), dies in London. A continent mourns its most beloved statesman.
Modern
2015
Magufuli Elected
John Magufuli wins the presidency, known as "The Bulldozer" for his anti-corruption drive and later controversial COVID stance.
Modern
2021
First Female President
Samia Suluhu Hassan becomes Tanzania's first female president after Magufuli's death — a historic moment for the country and Africa.
